## Headline patterns

When you owe 5–10 distinct headline angles, return real angles — not paraphrases. Here are patterns that produce genuinely different headlines for the same product.

### Angles, not paraphrases

A paraphrase: "Save time on email" / "Spend less time on email" / "Reduce email time".
Real angles: "Stop typing the same reply twice" (mechanism) / "Inbox zero by lunch" (outcome) / "The autocomplete that knows your tone" (category) / "For people who hate templates" (audience) / "Built by ex-Gmail engineers" (proof).

### Pattern catalogue

- **Outcome** — name the after-state. "Inbox zero by lunch."
- **Mechanism** — name the how. "Stop typing the same reply twice."
- **Audience** — name the reader. "For people who hate templates."
- **Category-of-one** — name the new category. "The autocomplete that knows your tone."
- **Proof** — name the credibility. "Built by ex-Gmail engineers."
- **Number** — name the magnitude. "12 minutes saved per email."
- **Contrast** — name what it isn't. "Email replies, without the templates."
- **Question** — name the reader's pain. "Why are you still typing the same reply?"
- **Command** — name the action. "Reply once. Forever."
- **Status** — name the identity shift. "The way fast teams email."

### Cuts that earn their place

- Adjectives must do work: "fast" alone is filler; "12-minute" is a number.
- Nouns over verbs: "Reply" beats "Replies that are fast".
- Specific over general: "ex-Gmail engineers" beats "industry veterans".
- Concrete over abstract: "by lunch" beats "in less time".

### Common failures to avoid

- All 5 headlines are the same angle reworded.
- Adjective stacking: "The smart, fast, easy way…".
- Cleverness with no payoff — wordplay that doesn't communicate the value.
- Lead with the brand instead of the value.
- Asking a question the reader can answer "no" to.

### Length

- **Page heroes**: 4–8 words.
- **Email subjects**: 5–9 words; avoid all-caps, avoid two emojis, avoid "RE:" fakes.
- **Ad headlines**: hit the platform's character cap with one to spare.

### When to push back

- If the brief gives no audience or no value prop, don't write 10 headlines — write zero, ask for the missing input. Vague briefs make vague copy.
